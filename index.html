Want To Help San Francisco?
Good, we could use your help.

We are a Code for America Brigade, which means we're volunteers that contribute our talents toward improving the way our local governments and community organizations use the web. Brigade is about action and you're here because you are someone who takes action.

We do a lot of things together, but our main activity is our Hack Night, which is held every Wednesday night from 6:30 to 9PM. The Hack Night is for those who want to:

  • get work done on civic projects
  • start a new project, or find one to join (all backgrounds welcome!)
  • bounce ideas and get help from tech and gov experts
  • learn about open data, smart cities, and open government

For more See what kind of things brigades do, or watch the inspirational TED talk given by Jennifer Pahlka, the founder of Code for America.

Bring the hack night to your city

Hack nights anchor and grow volunteer civic hacking communities while avoiding 'hackathon fatigue'. To learn how to organize one in your city, see the Code for America blog.

Friends have forked the hack night in many cities:

  • Boston, MA
  • Hampton Roads, VA
  • London, UK
  • Miami, FL
  • New York, NY
  • Oakland, CA
  • Philadelphia, PA
  • Raleigh / Durham, NC
  • Chicago, IL
  • Washington, DC
